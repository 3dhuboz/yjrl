# Child Safety Incident Playbook

This playbook is for Yeppoon Junior Rugby League administrators handling reports from the app. It is an operational guide, not legal advice. The club committee should review it against current QRL/NRL, Queensland child safe, Blue Card, police, and eSafety requirements before launch.

## Core Rules

- If a child is in immediate danger, call 000 before using the app workflow.
- Take every grooming, predatory behaviour, sexualised contact, bullying, harassment, image misuse, or boundary concern seriously.
- Preserve evidence before deleting or changing content unless leaving it live creates immediate risk.
- Do not investigate by confronting a child, alleged perpetrator, or reporter in chat.
- Keep report access limited to authorised club leaders with a genuine need to know.
- Where a report involves an adult role holder, suspend or reject the adult approval in the app while the club determines the next step.

## Severity Levels

### Critical

Use for immediate danger, suspected grooming, child sexual abuse material, credible abuse allegations, threats, or adult boundary violations.

Actions:
- Call 000 if there is immediate danger.
- Close off the app pathway: suspend adult access, remove team coach assignment, reject or quarantine media as needed.
- Preserve message/upload/profile evidence using screenshots, exported records, and the app audit trail.
- Escalate to the nominated Child Safety Officer, club president, and relevant external body.
- Notify parents or guardians when safe and appropriate.

### High

Use for repeated unwanted contact, targeted harassment, harmful image sharing, non-urgent adult boundary concerns, or public exposure of child personal information.

Actions:
- Triage in the admin Safety Reports table.
- Preserve evidence and record action notes.
- Remove or reject public content where it exposes a child.
- Temporarily suspend adult or coach access if risk is plausible.
- Decide whether external reporting is required.

### Medium

Use for bullying, inappropriate language, accidental disclosure, parent/player disputes, or repeated chat rule breaches.

Actions:
- Triage the report.
- Record who reviewed it and the action taken.
- Warn, mute, or remove content where appropriate.
- Monitor repeat behaviour.

### Low

Use for mistaken reports, unclear concerns, or minor moderation issues.

Actions:
- Review and close with a short action note.
- Keep the audit trail intact.

## App Workflow

1. Open `Admin -> Chat Safety`.
2. Review the report reason, description, reporter, entity, severity, and related audit entries.
3. If the report relates to media, use Upload Review to approve only safe and consented media, or reject it to remove the object from R2.
4. If the report relates to an adult, use Adult Role Approvals to reject, expire, or suspend the adult role. The app revokes coach access and clears coach team assignment when appropriate.
5. If the report relates to a player/team assignment, confirm the player is linked to the correct parent and team.
6. Add a clear action note and mark the report triaged, actioned, or closed.
7. Record any external report reference numbers outside the app until a formal incident module exists.

## Evidence Handling

- Keep screenshots or exports in a restricted club folder.
- Do not repeatedly circulate distressing material.
- Do not download or redistribute illegal child abuse material. Preserve the report context and seek police/platform advice.
- Keep a simple timeline: who reported, who reviewed, what was found, what was removed, who was notified, and what external reports were made.

## Launch Sign-Off

Before opening the app to paying families, the club should nominate:

- Child Safety Officer.
- Backup reviewer.
- Committee escalation contact.
- Parent communication owner.
- External reporting owner.
- Data/evidence storage location.
